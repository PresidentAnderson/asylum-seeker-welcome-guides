## First 24-48 Hours

### Immediate Priorities
- [ ] Find safe accommodation
- [ ] Register with immigration authorities
- [ ] Access emergency healthcare if needed
- [ ] Contact legal aid services
- [ ] Secure basic necessities

### Essential Documents to Obtain
- Refugee Protection Claimant Document
- Social Insurance Number (SIN) - temporary
- Provincial health card (waiting period may apply)
- Photo ID from provincial government

## Legal Rights and Status

### Refugee Claim Process
1. **Port of Entry Claim**: Made at border crossing or airport
2. **Inland Claim**: Made at IRCC office if already in Canada
3. **Basis of Claim Form**: Must be submitted within 15 days
4. **Hearing**: Immigration and Refugee Board (IRB) hearing

### Your Rights
- Right to legal representation
- Right to an interpreter
- Right to appeal negative decisions
- Protection from detention (except specific circumstances)
- Access to essential services

### Free Legal Aid
- **Legal Aid Ontario**: 1-800-668-8258
- **Legal Aid BC**: 1-866-577-2525
- **Legal Aid Alberta**: 1-866-845-3425
- **Commission des services juridiques (Quebec)**: 514-873-3562

## Healthcare Access

### Interim Federal Health Program (IFHP)
- Coverage for basic healthcare
- Prescription medications
- Emergency dental and vision care
- Mental health services

### How to Access
1. Register with IFHP through your refugee claim
2. Find IFHP-approved providers
3. Present your refugee claimant document

### Provincial Coverage
- Waiting period: 3 months in most provinces
- Apply as soon as eligible
- Coverage varies by province

## Housing and Shelter

### Emergency Shelter
- Call 211 for shelter information
- Red Cross temporary housing program
- Municipal shelter systems
- Faith-based organizations

### Transitional Housing
- Refugee housing programs
- Subsidized housing wait lists
- Room rentals and shared accommodation
- Housing allowance programs

### Tenant Rights
- Protection from discrimination
- Right to safe, maintained housing
- Proper eviction procedures must be followed
- Deposits limited to last month's rent

## Education

### Children (K-12)
- **Enrollment**: Free and mandatory for ages 6-16
- **Requirements**: Proof of age, immunization records
- **ESL Support**: Available in most school districts
- **Settlement Workers**: Available in schools (SWIS program)

### Adults
- Language training (LINC/CLIC programs) - free
- High school equivalency programs
- College and university access (fees may apply)
- Skills training and bridging programs

## Employment

### Work Permit
- Can apply once refugee claim is submitted
- Processing time: 2-4 months
- Valid until claim decision
- Renewable if claim under appeal

### Job Search Resources
- Job Bank Canada: jobbank.gc.ca
- Settlement agencies' employment programs
- Provincial employment centers
- Credential recognition services

### Worker Rights
- Minimum wage protection
- Workplace safety standards
- Employment standards (hours, breaks, overtime)
- Protection from discrimination
